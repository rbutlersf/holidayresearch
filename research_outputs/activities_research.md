### Venice Day Trip (1 day recommended)

#### Must-Do Experiences

**1. St. Mark's Basilica**
- **What:** Iconic Byzantine church, UNESCO site, 900+ years old, stunning interior mosaics and artwork
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - 9-year-old friendly, though may need sitting breaks due to crowds
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - Basic entry: €6 per person = €42 for 7 people
  - With Pala d'Oro (golden altar): €12 = €84
  - With Museum & Loggia: €15 = €105
  - Skip-the-line (third-party): €18-20 = $126-140 per person
- **Advance Booking:** RECOMMENDED 1-2 weeks ahead via GetYourGuide or official site to skip 30-45 minute lines
- **Booking Links:**
  - Official: https://saintmarksbasilica.com/
  - Skip-the-line: https://www.getyourguide.com/venice-l35/venice-st-mark-s-basilica-skip-the-line-tickets-t645294/
- **Notes:** Arrive before 9:30am or after 4pm for smaller crowds. Dress code enforced (covered shoulders/knees). Free entry before official opening (7am) but interior dim. Can go inside without full ticket to see main hall briefly.

**2. Doge's Palace (Palazzo Ducale)**
- **What:** Former residence/office of Venetian rulers, Renaissance architecture, Bridge of Sighs connection, prison museums
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - older teens enjoy history/architecture, 9-year-old may tire from walking
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - Adult ticket: €25 (30+ days advance) or €30 (walk-up)
  - Reduced (kids 6-14): €13 (advance) or €15 (walk-up)
  - 7 people mixed ages: ~€140-160
  - Skip-the-line add-on: €5-10 per person
- **Advance Booking:** YES - book 30+ days ahead to get €5 discount. Book via https://palazzoducale.visitmuve.it/en/pianifica-la-tua-visita/tickets/
- **Notes:** Includes secret itineraries tour of prison (very popular, books out months ahead). Standard tour sufficient for families. Combination ticket with other museums available.

**3. Gondola Ride**
- **What:** Traditional flat-bottomed boat ride through Grand Canal and smaller waterways, romantic/iconic Venice experience
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - all ages, though 30-40 min may feel long to impatient child
- **Duration:** 30-40 minutes typically
- **Pricing (Official Fixed Rates):**
  - Private gondola (max 5 people): €90 daytime, €110 evening (7pm+)
  - For 7 people: need 2 gondolas = €180 daytime or €220 evening
  - **Better budget option:** Shared gondola tour €30-40 per person = €210-280 for 7
  - Traghetto (ferry crossing only): €2 per person = €14 (not scenic but cheap)
- **Advance Booking:** Can book day-of, but booking 1-2 days ahead via GetYourGuide secures better times (sunset slots book out)
- **Booking Link:** https://www.getyourguide.com/venice-l35/gondola-tours-tc264/
- **Notes:** Daytime is cheaper and less crowded than evening. Do NOT book expensive gondola directly at Piazza San Marco (tourist trap). Shared tours offer better value for families.

**4. Rialto Bridge & Markets (FREE)**
- **What:** Historic bridge with covered market, produce/souvenir shopping, photo opportunities, free to walk
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - kids enjoy shopping and street food
- **Duration:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Pricing:** FREE (but plan €5-10 per person for snacks/souvenirs)
- **Notes:** Very crowded 11am-4pm. Best visited early morning or evening. Fresh produce, cheeses, seafood on lower level. Tourist stalls upstairs. Street food options: panini, spritz, arancini €5-8.

**5. Murano Glass Factory Tour**
- **What:** See traditional Venetian glass-blowing demonstrations, learn island history, watch artisans create glassware
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - teens and 9-year-old fascinated by live demonstrations, educational
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours including demonstration and tour
- **Pricing:**
  - Glass factory tour with demo: €15-25 per person = €105-175
  - Hands-on workshop add: €30-40 per person extra
  - Family option recommended: tour + demo without workshop
- **Advance Booking:** NOT required for basic tour, but book 1-2 days ahead for workshop guarantees best times
- **Booking Link:** https://www.viator.com/tours/Venice/Glass-Factory-Tour-with-Glass-Blowing-Demonstration/d522-9555P3
- **Notes:** Factories open daily. Shop browsing is high-pressure (polite but pushy sales). Demonstrations are genuine and impressive. Family tour versions available. Non-alcoholic drinks should be available for kids despite tours including beverages.

**6. Wander Streets & Get Lost (FREE)**
- **What:** Unstructured exploration, canal walks, bridge crossing, discovering neighborhoods beyond tourist areas
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - adventure and discovery appealing to all ages
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Pricing:** FREE (budget for gelato, coffee, water: €3-5 per person = €21-35)
- **Notes:** Best strategy: start in Dorsoduro or Cannaregio districts, aim toward something vaguely geographical (like a church), enjoy the journey. Bring water and good shoes. Many areas quieter and more authentic than San Marco.

### Florence Day Trip (1 day recommended)

#### Must-Do Experiences

**1. Uffizi Gallery** ⭐ PRIORITY BOOKING
- **What:** World's greatest Renaissance art collection - works by Botticelli, Leonardo, Michelangelo, Raphael; 2,700+ artworks in 45 rooms
- **Age-appropriate:** Moderate - older teens very interested, middle teens may fatigue, 9-year-old needs breaks, ~90 min per person ideal
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours (depending on interest level)
- **Pricing:**
  - Adult: €25 (as of 2026)
  - Reduced (kids under 18): €17
  - 7 people estimate: ~€140-160
  - Skip-the-line booking fee: €2-4 extra
- **Advance Booking:** CRITICAL - Book 4-6 weeks ahead (timed entry required)
  - As of October 2025, new booking platform is https://tickets.uffizi.it/
  - Book the moment you finalize Florence date
  - After 4pm entry discounted to €16
- **Booking Links:**
  - Official (required): https://tickets.uffizi.it/
  - Skip-the-line alternative: https://www.viator.com/tours/Florence/Skip-the-Line-Florence-Uffizi-Gallery-Tickets/d519-2428TU
- **Notes:** Allow afternoon entry to save €9 per person if schedule flexible. Non-transferable with photo ID required. Tickets are digital (print or show on phone).

**2. Accademia Gallery (Michelangelo's David)**
- **What:** See Michelangelo's 17-foot marble David statue, Renaissance paintings, sculpture collection
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - David is globally recognized, kids find it iconic and impressive
- **Duration:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - Standard: ~€13-15 per person
  - 7 people: ~€100-105
- **Advance Booking:** YES - book 3-4 weeks ahead, timed entry required. Same platform as Uffizi (https://tickets.uffizi.it/) or call +39-055-294-883
- **Booking Link:** https://www.accademia.org/buy-tickets/
- **Notes:** MUST see David - once-in-a-lifetime statue. Can feel touristy/crowded. Go early morning (8:30am) or late afternoon (4pm) for fewer crowds. Takes 10-15 min to see David properly, rest of museum flexible.

**3. Florence Duomo & Dome Climb**
- **What:** Fourth-largest cathedral in world, iconic red dome, climb 463 steps to breathtaking views, Baptistry
- **Age-appropriate:** 9-year-old CAN do climb but challenging - must be reasonably fit, no elevator
- **Duration:**
  - Exterior/Baptistry: 30-45 min
  - Dome climb: 45-60 min (strenuous)
- **Pricing:**
  - Duomo exterior: FREE
  - Dome climb: €30 adult, €12 kids 7-14 (pay per activity)
  - Full Brunelleschi Pass (all sites): €30-35
  - 7 people doing dome climb: ~€140-170
- **Advance Booking:** YES - dome entry slots fill weeks ahead. Timed slots mandatory. Book at https://duomo.firenze.it/en/720/brunelleschi-pass or via GetYourGuide
- **Notes:** Dome climb is genuinely difficult - claustrophobic sections, narrow staircases, steep. 9-year-old manageable if athletic. Not for those afraid of heights. Reserve-only, cannot change times once booked. Book at least 2-3 weeks ahead.

**4. Gelato-Making Class (Family-Friendly)**
- **What:** Learn to make authentic Italian gelato from scratch, then taste your creations
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - all ages love hands-on cooking and eating gelato
- **Duration:** 2 hours typically
- **Pricing:**
  - €45-65 per person
  - 7 people: €315-455
  - Often discounts for groups 5+
- **Advance Booking:** 1-2 weeks advance recommended (high season books out)
- **Booking Links:**
  - GetYourGuide: https://www.getyourguide.com/florence-l32/florence-gelato-making-class-t214593/
  - LivTours: https://livtours.com/tours/gelato-making-class-in-florence-semi-private/
  - Mama Florence: https://www.mamaflorence.com/private-gelato-and-biscotti-cooking-class
- **Notes:** Afternoon timing (3-5pm) works well with day trip schedule. Combines pasta-making + gelato to save money. Classes in English. Soft skill-building, relaxing alternative to museum-heavy day. Great for mixed age groups.

**5. Mercato Centrale (Central Market) - Food Hall**
- **What:** Three-level food market - ground floor fresh produce (closes 2pm), upper floors food court with 20+ stalls, authentic atmosphere
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - kids love food discovery, crowded but family-friendly
- **Duration:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - FREE to walk/browse
  - Meals: €6-12 per plate (fresh pasta €6, prepared dishes €8-10)
  - Budget: €60-80 total for group snacking/lunch
- **Advance Booking:** NOT needed (busy all day)
- **Booking Link:** https://www.mercatocentrale.com/florence/
- **Notes:** Go before 12:30pm for ground floor produce. Upper level food court quieter on 3rd floor. Quality-to-price ratio excellent. Great for quick lunch between museums.

**6. Ponte Vecchio & Leather Market (FREE to browse)**
- **What:** Medieval bridge with jewelry shops, river views, leather goods market in adjacent streets
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - good for shopping-interested family members
- **Duration:** 45-60 min
- **Pricing:**
  - FREE to walk
  - Budget €10-20 per person for souvenirs if interested
- **Notes:** Extremely touristy and crowded (11am-5pm). Shop prices inflated. Better for casual browsing than serious purchases. San Lorenzo leather market across river more authentic and cheaper.

### Parma Day Trip (1 half or full day recommended)

#### Must-Do Experiences

**1. Parmigiano-Reggiano Factory Tour** ⭐ ESSENTIAL EXPERIENCE
- **What:** Visit working cheese factory, learn production process, watch cheese-makers, taste fresh Parmigiano
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - kids fascinated by food production, sensory experience, tasty samples
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - €15-25 per person depending on facility
  - 7 people: €105-175
  - Some facilities include tasting, others charge separate
- **Advance Booking:** YES - 3-4 days to 1 week ahead
  - Bio Reggiani (family-friendly): https://bioreggiani.com/en/product/bioreggiani-guided-tour/
  - Società Agricola Saliceto: https://www.saliceto.com/en/visit-cheese-factory/
  - San Pier Damiani: https://www.sanpierdamiani.com/en/guided-tours/
  - Official listing: https://www.parmigianoreggiano.com/dairies-book-a-guided-tour
- **Notes:** Tours offered 9am-6pm typically. Children 6-11 at some facilities get special pricing (Bio Reggiani €11 per child). Free for under 6. Most tours in Italian with English translation available if arranged ahead. Parking easy. Factory shop for purchasing.

**2. Prosciutto di Parma Producer Visit**
- **What:** See Parma ham production, aging cellars, tasting
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - less "factory" than cheese, more elegant ham aging process, good for older kids
- **Duration:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - €10-20 per person with tasting
  - 7 people: €70-140
- **Advance Booking:** 2-3 weeks via https://www.parmigianoreggiano.com/dairies-book-a-guided-tour or local guides
- **Notes:** Often combined with Parmigiano tour (half-day package available €30-40/person). Less crowded than cheese factory. Better for teens; younger child may find aging cellars less interesting.

**3. Food-Focused Walking Tour of Parma**
- **What:** Guided culinary history tour, market visits, food tastings, restaurant recommendations
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - educational and delicious
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - €40-60 per person with food sampling
  - 7 people: €280-420
- **Advance Booking:** 1-2 weeks via Viator: https://www.viator.com/tours/Parma/Parmigiano-cheese-and-Parma-ham-Tour/d27234-208820P1
- **Notes:** Excellent introduction to Parma food culture. Covers markets, restaurants, food shops. Often includes antipasto sampling. English-speaking guides available.

**4. Parma Cathedral & Baptistry**
- **What:** Beautiful medieval cathedral with frescoed dome, octagonal baptistry, historic square
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - Renaissance architecture appreciation, not overly crowded
- **Duration:** 45-60 min
- **Pricing:**
  - Cathedral: FREE
  - Baptistry: €3-5 per person (~€21-35 total)
  - Camera: €2 per person for dome decoration viewing (~€14)
- **Advance Booking:** NOT needed
- **Notes:** Much less crowded than Florence duomo. Good midday activity. Dome frescoes beautiful. Square (Piazza del Duomo) charming and manageable.

**5. Parma Food Market (Mercato di Mezzo)** (FREE to browse)
- **What:** Traditional covered market, fresh produce, meats, cheeses, local specialties
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - sensory experience, food education
- **Duration:** 30-45 min
- **Pricing:** FREE (budget €3-8 per person if sampling)
- **Notes:** Best visited 9-11am before peak hours. Authentic local experience, less touristy than Venice markets. Good place to buy snacks/picnic items.

### Milan Day Trip (1 full day recommended)

#### Must-Do Experiences

**1. Last Supper (Il Cenacolo) by Leonardo da Vinci** ⭐⭐⭐ HIGHEST PRIORITY
- **What:** Leonardo's masterpiece mural (1497) in monastery refectory - one of world's greatest artworks
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - historically/artistically significant, moving, though 9-year-old may need context explanation
- **Duration:** 30-45 min (timed entry limits time)
- **Pricing:**
  - €10-12 per person
  - 7 people: €70-84
  - Advance booking fee: €2 per person included
- **Advance Booking:** ⭐⭐⭐ CRITICAL - BOOK THE MOMENT TICKETS RELEASE
  - Tickets released quarterly (Feb-Apr window opens early March)
  - You need the MAY-JULY 2026 window
  - Book the DAY tickets become available (typically Wed at 12pm Italian time)
  - Booking link: https://cenacolovinciano.org/en/info/
  - Alternative: https://www.viator.com/Milan-attractions/Da-Vincis-Last-Supper-Il-Cenacolo/d512-a727
- **Notes:**
  - Peak summer (July) sells out 90+ days ahead
  - Set phone reminders for exactly when booking window opens
  - This is NOT negotiable - do not delay
  - Timed slots strictly enforced (arrive 10 min early)
  - Photography forbidden
  - Maximum 15 min inside per group
  - Dress code: shoulders covered, knees covered

**2. Milan Duomo (Cathedral) & Rooftop Terrace**
- **What:** 4th largest cathedral globally, 600-year project, dramatic Gothic architecture, rooftop offers panoramic views
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - visually stunning, rooftop views thrilling for all ages
- **Duration:**
  - Interior only: 45-60 min
  - Interior + rooftop: 1.5-2 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - Cathedral interior: €8-10 per person
  - Rooftop by stairs: €12 per person
  - Rooftop by elevator (easier for 9-year-old): €15 per person
  - Combo rooftop ticket: €18-20 per person
  - Family combo (2 adults + 2 kids): €62.50 stairs / €75 elevator
  - Full family (2 adults + kids): Total 7 people ~€100-120
- **Advance Booking:** Recommended 3-5 days ahead to ensure availability and avoid lines
  - https://duomodimilanotickets.com/rooftop-tickets/
  - GetYourGuide: https://www.getyourguide.com/milan-l139/duomo-and-terraces-ticket-with-audioguide-t153354/
  - Tickets valid 2 days
- **Notes:** Rooftop by stairs cheaper but 80+ steps still required even with elevator. Interior allows stroller lockers (free). No baby strollers allowed inside building. Skip elevator if trying to save; stairs adequate for 9-year-old with breaks.

**3. Sforza Castle (Castello Sforzesco)** ⭐ Free/Low-Cost Option
- **What:** 15th-century castle complex, now museums, Leonardo's Sala delle Asse paintings, Michelangelo's Pietà, parks
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - kids enjoy castle exploration, rooms to explore, less crowded than Duomo
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - Castle exterior & courtyards: FREE
  - Museums entry: €5-8 per person (free for under 18 first & third Tuesdays after 2pm, first Sundays monthly)
  - 7 people with free admission: €0 (plan museum visit accordingly)
  - 7 people full price: ~€35-56
- **Advance Booking:** NOT needed (walk-up fine unless planning special exhibition)
- **Booking Link:** https://www.yesmilano.it/en/see-and-do/venues/castello-sforzesco
- **Notes:**
  - Free courtyards exploration essential - beautifully restored
  - Parco Sempione behind castle ideal for rest/picnic
  - Museum-quality without museum prices
  - Leonardo fans prioritize Sala delle Asse
  - Teens more interested than younger child

**4. Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II** (FREE to browse, €$ to shop)
- **What:** 19th-century shopping arcade, architectural marvel, luxury brands, historic establishment
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - good for window shopping, photos, architecture appreciation
- **Duration:** 30-45 min
- **Pricing:** FREE (but temptation to shop!)
- **Notes:** Extremely touristy. Espresso bar inside costs €8-10 (expensive but famous). Use bathrooms elsewhere. Teenagers may enjoy window shopping.

**5. Milan Science & Technology Museum (Museo Nazionale Scienza e Tecnologia Leonardo da Vinci)**
- **What:** Italy's largest science museum, hands-on exhibits, submarine tours (additional), Leonardo's original machines, kid workshops
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - all ages enjoy interactive exhibits, teens fascinated by Leonardo, 9-year-old appreciates hands-on areas
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours (more if doing submarine tour)
- **Pricing:**
  - Adult: €10
  - Reduced (kids 3-26): €7.50
  - Under 3: FREE
  - 7 people: ~€55
  - Submarine tour add-on: €5 per person (~€35)
- **Advance Booking:** NOT required (walk-up fine, though book if doing special workshops)
- **Booking Link:** https://www.yesmilano.it/en/see-and-do/venues/national-museum-science-and-technology-leonardo-da-vinci
- **Notes:**
  - Hours: Tues-Fri 9:30am-5pm, Sat-Sun 9:30am-6:30pm (closed Mondays)
  - Great rainy day option
  - Playlab for ages 3-6
  - Leonardo's machines in original building
  - Submarine in courtyard popular with kids

  - Strategy: visit 8-9am (free + fewer crowds)
  - Or 9:30pm (free but dark for photos)
  - Not "enclosed" - some people uncomfortable with heights
  - Stairs everywhere, no elevator (good exercise)
  - Parking available below

**3C. Matthias Church (Neighboring Bastion)**
- **What:** 14th-century Gothic church, ornate interior, bell tower climb possible
- **Age-appropriate:** YES
- **Duration:** 45-60 min
- **Pricing:** €7-10 per person entry
- **Notes:** Beautiful interior. Bell tower climb extra €3-5 per person. Less critical than Bastion.

#### 4. Parliament Building Tour

- **What:** Ornate neo-Gothic architecture (Hungarian parliament - second largest in Europe), guided interior tour, historic political site
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - impressive building, history lesson, good for older teens
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes guided tour (cannot enter alone)
- **Pricing:**
  - Guided tour: HUF 6,500 (~€17) per person
  - 7 people: HUF 45,500 (~€120)
- **Advance Booking:** YES - 2-3 weeks recommended (official: https://www.parlament.hu/web/visitors/purchasing-tickets)
  - Tours in English available multiple times daily
  - Peak summer books out
  - Family tickets available (1 adult + 1 child 0-14)
- **Booking Methods:**
  - Official: https://www.jegymester.hu/parlament
  - Third-party: Viator, GetYourGuide (often priced higher)
  - Visitor center in-person (not recommended in summer)
- **Notes:**
  - Photography forbidden inside (frustrating)
  - Exterior equally impressive at night (floodlit)
  - 45 min tour sufficient for families
  - Not essential if short on time (view from outside free)

#### 5. Pest Side - City Center

**5A. St. Stephen's Basilica**
- **What:** Budapest's most important church, neoclassical 1905, stunning interior, tower climb optional
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - architectural marvel
- **Duration:**
  - Interior: 30-45 min
  - With tower climb: 45-60 min
- **Pricing:**
  - Entry: €6-8 per person
  - Tower climb add: €3-4 per person extra
  - 7 people interior: €42-56
  - 7 people with tower: €63-84
- **Advance Booking:** NOT required
- **Notes:**
  - Tower climb 360 steps, good views
  - Holy right hand relic famous (in treasury room)
  - Beautiful organ music (timing dependent)

**5B. Great Market Hall (Vásárcsarnok)** - BEST FOOD EXPERIENCE

- **What:** Three-level historic market (opened 1897), ground floor fresh produce, upper food courts with 20+ stalls
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - sensory feast, food education, delicious
- **Duration:** 1-2 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - Entry: **FREE**
  - Meals: HUF 2,000-5,000 (~€5-12) per person for meal
  - Sampling: HUF 500-2,000 (~€1-5) per item
  - Budget: €30-50 for group tasting/light lunch
- **Advance Booking:** NOT required
- **Best Time:** 9am-12pm (before peak crowds, ground floor produce available)
- **Notes:**
  - Go with appetite - Hungarian goulash, strudel, paprika everything available
  - Kids under 14 often FREE on food tours (if booking)
  - Outside area with playground for younger child
  - Bathroom facilities available

**Food Tour Option:**
- Guided food tour: €30-50 per person including tastings
- 7 people: €210-350 (book 1-2 weeks)
- Book via: https://www.viator.com/tours/Budapest/Gourmet-Market-Walk-at-the-Great-Market-Hall-of-Budapest/d499-26581P2

**5C. Shoes on Danube Bank Memorial** (FREE)
- **What:** Holocaust memorial, 60 pairs of iron shoes representing victims shot into river (WWII), solemn/moving
- **Age-appropriate:** YES for older teens (sensitive but educational), 9-year-old might find disturbing
- **Duration:** 10-15 min
- **Pricing:** FREE
- **Notes:** Somber but important historical site. Brief visit between other activities. Excellent history lesson.

**5D. Váci Street & Shopping** (FREE to browse)
- **What:** Historic pedestrian street, shops, cafes, market stalls
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - good for shopping-interested family members
- **Duration:** 45-60 min
- **Pricing:** FREE (but temptation to shop!)
- **Notes:** Very touristy. Overpriced. More for browsing/atmosphere than shopping. Street performers common.

#### 6. Unique Experiences

**6A. Ruin Bars (Family-Friendly Daytime Visits)**

- **What:** Bohemian bars in abandoned buildings, vintage decor, young atmosphere (evening), some daytime-friendly
- **Age-appropriate:**
  - Evening/night: NOT family-friendly (adult bar scene)
  - Daytime (morning/early afternoon): Some venues family-appropriate
- **Top Family-Friendly Option: Kőleves Kert (Courtyard)**
  - Afternoon/early evening only: relaxed, courtyard, not bar-like
  - Best 2-5pm before evening crowds arrive
  - HUF 500-1,000 drink (~€1-3), can sit for hours with one drink
  - No booking needed
- **Alternative: Szimpla Farmers' Market (Sundays 9am-2pm only)**
  - Sunday market atmosphere, family-appropriate
  - NOT ruin bar vibe - more market
- **Notes:**
  - Stick to daytime, early timing (before 8pm)
  - Most ruin bars genuinely NOT family-friendly at night
  - Skip entirely if concerned

**6B. Margaret Island Bike Rental** ⭐ FAMILY ACTIVITY
- **What:** Car-free island in Danube, bike riding, parks, thermal baths, museums, 2.5km of car-free cycling
- **Age-appropriate:** YES - all ages for biking
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - Bike rental: HUF 3,000-5,000 (~€8-13) per person per hour
  - 7 people 2 hours: HUF 42,000-70,000 (~$110-185)
  - Kids bikes available
- **Advance Booking:**
  - NOT required (walk-up fine)
  - Go Mobility primary rental: https://gomobility.eu/category/margaret-island/?lang=en
  - Operating: March 1-November 30
  - Hours: 10am-sunset typically
- **Notes:**
  - Flat island, perfect for mixed ages
  - Multiple routes and speeds
  - Picnic opportunity
  - Playgrounds for younger child
  - Thermal baths on island (Palatinus)

**6C. Budapest Food Tours & Cooking Classes**
- **What:** Learn Hungarian cuisine, market tours, optional hands-on cooking
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Pricing:**
  - Market food tour: €30-50 per person
  - Cooking class: €50-80 per person
  - 7 people: €210-560 depending on type
- **Advance Booking:** 1-2 weeks
- **Options:**
  - https://www.viator.com/Budapest-attractions/Hungarian-Food-and-Wine-Tastings/d499-1165 (multiple options)
- **Notes:** Kids often FREE or discounted on food tours

**6D. House of Terror Museum** (TEEN-FOCUSED)
- **What:** WWII and Soviet occupation history, exhibits on Arrow Cross/KGB, adult themes
- **Age-appropriate:** Older teens YES (age 14+), sensitive history, intense subject matter, educational
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Pricing:** HUF 3,500-4,500 (~€9-12) per person
- **Notes:**
  - 9-year-old too young (disturbing content)
  - Older teenagers benefit from history lesson
  - English audio guides available
  - Plan for younger child separately during this visit

**6E. Retro/Quirky Museums**
- **Pinball Museum:** Playable vintage pinball machines, fun for teens
  - Pricing: €8-10 per person
  - Duration: 1-1.5 hours
  - Great rainy day option
- **Mini Museum:** Tiny museum of miniature exhibits
  - Pricing: €3-5 per person
  - Fun novelty visit

## Family Logistics Notes

**Energy & Attention Span:**
- Museums: 1.5-2 hour max before fatigue/boredom

- Food experiences: Extended (food = motivation!)

- Frequent snack breaks (every 2 hours)

- Adventure seekers: paragliding, canyon swing, high mountain access
